Our Process

1. Changed the Default Login

Changing the manufacturer's default login information prevents those with physical access to your router from potentially changing its settings. Once you are finished just exit the application, your changes have already been saved.

2. Changed SSID and Wi-Fi Password

By making this change you make it much more difficult for hackers to break into the network. Password cracking tables have a list of default passwords, so this was an important step.

3. Adjusted Features

Some router features are rarely used and may pose a threat to your network. Turning off features such as WPS will make your network more secure.

4. Verified Encryption

Making sure to use WPA2 saves your network from known vulnerabilities compared to other encryption methods.
